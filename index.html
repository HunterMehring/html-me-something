Florence, also known as "the eternal city", is a gorgeous city that has much more of a small city feel than Rome. The streets are much smaller, quieter, and cleaner. But just becasue the city is smaller does'nt mean that there isn't anything to do there. The city is full of amazing museums, food, and scenic views. For food i recommend the Mercato Centrale Firenze. This san-lorenzo-central-market was an old train station that has been converted into an eatery with gourmet restaurants on top and a market on the ground floor. All of it is very delicious.

There are a number of museums in florence to choose from. By far the most popular are the Accademia and the Uffizi galleries in the Accademia you will find many breathtaking pieces, but the crown jewel of the museum is the
Statue of David The uffizi is another amazing museum full of historical art. I recommend spending anywhere from half a day to a full day at both of these museums.

The centerpiece of the entire city is the Duomo which is a cathedral which opened it's doors in 1436. Because of height restrictions in the city, the Duomo towers over anything else in the city, and it is a marvel to look at anywhere you are in the city. Because of this, I recommend that you go to the Piazzale Michelangelo to see the best view in Florence. It is a bit of a walk, but the views are absolutely stunning; especially during sunset. To get to the Piazzale Michelangelo make sure to go across the old bridge(Ponte Veccio). The views from outside the bridge are much more impressive than once you actually go across. In years past there were markets and goldsmiths, but now there are just a bunch of overpriced jewlery shops.

Venice is an amazing city to go around and walk a little bit. It is easy to get lost in the streets, but that is ok, becasue there is always something you'll find. The cathedral in Venice is St Mark's Basilica The basilica is free, so that is always a great place to start.

The most traditional thing to do in Venice is to go on a gondola ride. However, those can cost you almost $100 for only a half hour. If you must go on a gondola ride, then make sure you do your research so you don't get ripped off.

Something that might be a little out of the ordinary is to go to the Dolomite Mountain range. This could easily be a day trip to get away from the city for just a little bit, and it is worth it. The views are truly stunning. There are a number of tours that you could take through the mountains.

I always like to get a good skyline view of the city that I'm in. Venice really only has one place where you can do this, the San Marco Bell Tower.

One last thing that I would recommend is to visit Murano, the glass blowing island. Murano is a little bit north of Venie, so you will have to take a short water taxi to get over there. Make sure you get there early becasue a lot of the shops there close early. If you are lucky enough you may see some of the owners working on some of their new art.
